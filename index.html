Yueyan Tang (唐乐言)

PhD candidate in Cognitive Science, University of California, San Diego

I study how infants and caregivers coordinate attention and communication in everyday environments. My work combines naturalistic behavioral observation, infant–wearable sensing, and computational modeling to characterize the dynamics of joint attention and early language input.

Currently, I am developing an open-source, multimodal infant wearable platform and analytic pipeline that enable scalable, high-fidelity recording of infant–caregiver interactions in the home. This work integrates time-series analysis, mixed-effects models, and machine learning to bridge developmental theory with practical tools for studying diverse and multilingual families.
